<p><b>Henderson</b> is an <a href="/wiki/Unincorporated_area" title="Unincorporated area">unincorporated community</a> and <a href="/wiki/Census-designated_place" title="Census-designated place">census-designated place</a> (CDP) in <a href="/wiki/Shiawassee_County,_Michigan" title="Shiawassee County, Michigan">Shiawassee County</a> in the <a href="/wiki/U.S._state" title="U.S. state">U.S. state</a> of <a href="/wiki/Michigan" title="Michigan">Michigan</a>.  The CDP had a population of 399 as of the <a href="/wiki/2010_United_States_Census" title="2010 United States Census">2010 census</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-MI_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MI-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The community is located within <a href="/wiki/Rush_Township,_Michigan" title="Rush Township, Michigan">Rush Township</a>.  Henderson was named for early settler John Henderson, and the community was formerly referred to as Hendersonville and Hazel Green.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Henderson,_Michigan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1836, Gideon Lee of New York City was first to enter the land here. Lee sold the land to Josiah Isham. Isham sold the land in 1858 to Andrew Henderson. In 1868, Andrew's son and community's name sake, John, built the first store in 1868 followed by the first hotel in 1875. The community became known as Hendersonville.<sup id="cite_ref-tap_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tap-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On May 14, 1868, the Hazel Green post office was opened here under postmaster William Cook. The Michigan Central Railroad station was called Henderson.<sup id="cite_ref-tap_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tap-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The post office was renamed on October 16, 1876 to Henderson. For Andrew Henderson, Ezra Mason platted the village in 1879.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>The community of Henderson was listed as a newly-organized <a href="/wiki/Census-designated_place" title="Census-designated place">census-designated place</a> for the <a href="/wiki/2010_United_States_Census" title="2010 United States Census">2010 census</a>, meaning it now has officially defined boundaries and population statistics for the first time.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Henderson,_Michigan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>According to the <a href="/wiki/U.S._Census_Bureau" class="mw-redirect" title="U.S. Census Bureau">U.S. Census Bureau</a>, the community has an area of 3.67 square miles (9.51&#160;km<sup>2</sup>), of which 3.60 square miles (9.32&#160;km<sup>2</sup>) is land and 0.07 square miles (0.18&#160;km<sup>2</sup>) (1.91%) is water.<sup id="cite_ref-MI_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MI-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Shiawassee_River" title="Shiawassee River">Shiawassee River</a> passes through the community, and <a href="/wiki/M-52_(Michigan_highway)" title="M-52 (Michigan highway)">M-52</a> forms part of the eastern border with <a href="/wiki/New_Haven_Township,_Shiawassee_County,_Michigan" title="New Haven Township, Shiawassee County, Michigan">New Haven Township</a>.  
</p>
