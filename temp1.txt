<p><b>York</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Town" title="Town">town</a> in <a href="/wiki/York_County,_Maine" title="York County, Maine">York County, Maine</a>, United States, near the southern tip of the state. The population in the <a href="/wiki/2010_United_States_Census" title="2010 United States Census">2010 census</a> was 12,529. Situated beside the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic Ocean</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Maine" title="Gulf of Maine">Gulf of Maine</a>, York is a well-known summer resort town. It is home to three 18-hole golf clubs, four sandy beaches, and <a href="/wiki/Agamenticus" class="mw-redirect" title="Agamenticus">Mount Agamenticus</a>. It includes the villages of York Village, <a href="/wiki/York_Harbor,_Maine" title="York Harbor, Maine">York Harbor</a>, <a href="/wiki/York_Beach,_Maine" title="York Beach, Maine">York Beach</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cape_Neddick,_Maine" title="Cape Neddick, Maine">Cape Neddick</a>.
</p><p>York is part of the <a href="/wiki/Portland,_Maine" title="Portland, Maine">Portland</a>–<a href="/wiki/South_Portland,_Maine" title="South Portland, Maine">South Portland</a>–<a href="/wiki/Biddeford,_Maine" title="Biddeford, Maine">Biddeford</a>, Maine <a href="/wiki/Portland-South_Portland-Biddeford_metropolitan_area" class="mw-redirect" title="Portland-South Portland-Biddeford metropolitan area">metropolitan statistical area</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=York,_Maine&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:The_Old_Gaol,_York_Village,_ME.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/12/The_Old_Gaol%2C_York_Village%2C_ME.jpg/220px-The_Old_Gaol%2C_York_Village%2C_ME.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="158" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/12/The_Old_Gaol%2C_York_Village%2C_ME.jpg/330px-The_Old_Gaol%2C_York_Village%2C_ME.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/12/The_Old_Gaol%2C_York_Village%2C_ME.jpg/440px-The_Old_Gaol%2C_York_Village%2C_ME.jpg 2x" data-file-width="686" data-file-height="493" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:The_Old_Gaol,_York_Village,_ME.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/The_Old_Gaol" class="mw-redirect" title="The Old Gaol">The Old Gaol</a> (Jail) in 1901</div></div></div>
<p>First settled by Europeans in 1624, the plantation was originally called Agamenticus, the <a href="/wiki/Abenaki" title="Abenaki">Abenaki</a> term for the <a href="/wiki/York_River_(Maine)" title="York River (Maine)">York River</a>. In 1638, settlers changed the name to Bristol after <a href="/wiki/Bristol,_England" class="mw-redirect" title="Bristol, England">Bristol</a>, <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>, from which they had immigrated. Envisioning a great city arising from the <a href="/wiki/Wilderness" title="Wilderness">wilderness</a>, Sir <a href="/wiki/Ferdinando_Gorges" title="Ferdinando Gorges">Ferdinando Gorges</a>, lord proprietor of Maine under the <a href="/wiki/Plymouth_Council_for_New_England" class="mw-redirect" title="Plymouth Council for New England">Plymouth patent</a>, named the capital of his province Gorgeana. On March 1, 1642, by charter of <a href="/wiki/King_Charles_I_of_England" class="mw-redirect" title="King Charles I of England">King Charles I</a>, Gorgeana became the first incorporated city in America.<sup id="cite_ref-Coolidge_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Coolidge-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Following Gorges' death, the <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts_Bay_Colony" title="Massachusetts Bay Colony">Massachusetts Bay Colony</a> claimed his dominion. In 1652, York, Massachusetts, was incorporated from a portion of Gorgeana, making it the second oldest town in Maine after <a href="/wiki/Kittery,_Maine" title="Kittery, Maine">Kittery</a>, incorporated two years earlier. It was named for <a href="/wiki/York" title="York">York</a>, England. But control of the region was contested between <a href="/wiki/New_England" title="New England">New England</a> and <a href="/wiki/New_France" title="New France">New France</a>, which incited <a href="/wiki/Native_Americans_in_the_United_States" title="Native Americans in the United States">Native Americans</a> to attack <a href="/wiki/English_people" title="English people">English</a> settlements throughout the <a href="/wiki/French_and_Indian_Wars" title="French and Indian Wars">French and Indian Wars</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Coolidge_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Coolidge-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Sewall%27s_Map_of_York,_Maine,_1794.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/74/Sewall%27s_Map_of_York%2C_Maine%2C_1794.png/220px-Sewall%27s_Map_of_York%2C_Maine%2C_1794.png" decoding="async" width="220" height="318" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/74/Sewall%27s_Map_of_York%2C_Maine%2C_1794.png/330px-Sewall%27s_Map_of_York%2C_Maine%2C_1794.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/74/Sewall%27s_Map_of_York%2C_Maine%2C_1794.png/440px-Sewall%27s_Map_of_York%2C_Maine%2C_1794.png 2x" data-file-width="1719" data-file-height="2488" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Sewall%27s_Map_of_York,_Maine,_1794.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Sewall's Map of York, Maine, 1794</div></div></div>
<p>The first Congregational church of York was organized in 1672, by Rev. <a href="/wiki/Shubael_Dummer" title="Shubael Dummer">Shubael Dummer</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> the son of <a href="/wiki/Richard_Dummer" title="Richard Dummer">Richard Dummer</a> and uncle to <a href="/wiki/William_Dummer" title="William Dummer">William Dummer</a>, who became acting governor of the <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Massachusetts_Bay" title="Province of Massachusetts Bay">Province of Massachusetts Bay</a>.
</p><p>During <a href="/wiki/King_William%27s_War" title="King William&#39;s War">King William's War</a>, York was destroyed in the <a href="/wiki/Candlemas_Massacre" class="mw-redirect" title="Candlemas Massacre">Candlemas Massacre</a> of 1692. During the raid by the Abenakis, Dummer was shot at his own front door. About 50 others were slain and near 100 carried away captive, among them Dummer's wife, Lydia, and their son, where "through snows and hardships among those dragons of the desert she also quickly died"; nothing further was heard of the boy.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The final local Indian attack occurred at the Cape Neddick area during <a href="/wiki/Dummer%27s_War" title="Dummer&#39;s War">Dummer's War</a> in 1723. Hostilities diminished with the <a href="/wiki/French_people" title="French people">French</a> defeat at the <a href="/wiki/Siege_of_Louisbourg_(1745)" title="Siege of Louisbourg (1745)">Siege of Louisbourg (1745)</a>, and ceased altogether with the 1763 <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Paris_(1763)" title="Treaty of Paris (1763)">Treaty of Paris</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Trading_center">Trading center</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=York,_Maine&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Trading center">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As provincial capital and site of the <a href="/wiki/The_Old_Gaol" class="mw-redirect" title="The Old Gaol">Royal Gaol</a> (Jail), York prospered. Numerous <a href="/wiki/Wharf" title="Wharf">wharves</a> and <a href="/wiki/Warehouse" title="Warehouse">warehouses</a> serviced trade with the <a href="/wiki/West_Indies" title="West Indies">West Indies</a>. Agricultural products and <a href="/wiki/Lumber" title="Lumber">lumber</a> were shipped in exchange for <a href="/wiki/Sugar" title="Sugar">sugar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Molasses" title="Molasses">molasses</a> and other commodities. One notable <a href="/wiki/Merchant" title="Merchant">merchant</a> was <a href="/wiki/John_Hancock" title="John Hancock">John Hancock</a>, whose establishment is now a museum.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Following the <a href="/wiki/American_Revolutionary_War" title="American Revolutionary War">Revolution</a>, however, President <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Jefferson" title="Thomas Jefferson">Thomas Jefferson's</a> <a href="/wiki/Embargo_Act_of_1807" title="Embargo Act of 1807">Embargo Act of 1807</a> crippled trade. York, bereft of status as capitol, would not again be prosperous until after the <a href="/wiki/American_Civil_War" title="American Civil War">Civil War</a>, when its sea breezes and colonial charm, including old homes like the <a href="/wiki/John_Sedgley_Homestead" title="John Sedgley Homestead">John Sedgley Homestead</a>, attracted tourists.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Present_day">Present day</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=York,_Maine&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Present day">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Like <a href="/wiki/Bar_Harbor,_Maine" title="Bar Harbor, Maine">Bar Harbor</a> and <a href="/wiki/Newport,_Rhode_Island" title="Newport, Rhode Island">Newport</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rhode_Island" title="Rhode Island">Rhode Island</a>, York became a fashionable summer resort, and retains many distinctive examples of <a href="/wiki/Gilded_Age" title="Gilded Age">Gilded Age</a> architecture, particularly in the <a href="/wiki/Shingle_style" class="mw-redirect" title="Shingle style">Shingle style</a>. A cluster of historic buildings in the center of York Village are maintained as museums by the Old York Historical Society.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Yorks">The Yorks</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=York,_Maine&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: The Yorks">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Cape_Neddick_Light_US-ME.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ab/Cape_Neddick_Light_US-ME.jpg/220px-Cape_Neddick_Light_US-ME.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ab/Cape_Neddick_Light_US-ME.jpg/330px-Cape_Neddick_Light_US-ME.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ab/Cape_Neddick_Light_US-ME.jpg/440px-Cape_Neddick_Light_US-ME.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2048" data-file-height="1365" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Cape_Neddick_Light_US-ME.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Cape_Neddick_Light" title="Cape Neddick Light">Cape Neddick Light</a></div></div></div>
<ul><li>York Village — including the historic structures, and upscale shops</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/York_Harbor,_Maine" title="York Harbor, Maine">York Harbor</a> — with a number of elegant inns, historic homes and large estates</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/York_Beach,_Maine" title="York Beach, Maine">York Beach</a> — with popular attractions such as arcades, souvenir shops and stores</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Cape_Neddick,_Maine" title="Cape Neddick, Maine">Cape Neddick</a> — mainly residences, best known for its lighthouse</li></ul>
<p>During summer months, summer residents and tourists (chiefly families) throng Short Sands Beach, which is in the district of <a href="/wiki/York_Beach,_Maine" title="York Beach, Maine">York Beach</a> itself, as well as Long Sands Beach, the town's longest with more than a mile of sand stretching between York Beach and York Harbor. Dozens of five-star hotels and other accommodations operate in the York Beach area, although most close after summer.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2020)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>Many spots throughout The Yorks have picturesque views of the famous <a href="/wiki/Cape_Neddick_Light" title="Cape Neddick Light">Cape Neddick Light</a> at Nubble Rock, which has figured in both artists' work and souvenirs of the Maine coast. A photo of the Cape Neddick Light is on the <a href="/wiki/Voyager_1" title="Voyager 1">Voyager 1</a> spacecraft labeled as Seashore, Maine.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Visible in clear weather is the 133 foot (40 meter) tall <a href="/wiki/Boon_Island_Light" title="Boon Island Light">Boon Island Light</a> on <a href="/wiki/Boon_Island" title="Boon Island">Boon Island</a>, located 6.2 miles (10.0&#160;km) off York. Old-fashioned restaurants, like the Goldenrod, maintain the historic character of the York Beach area.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2020)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=York,_Maine&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>According to the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Census_Bureau" title="United States Census Bureau">United States Census Bureau</a>, the town has a total area of 131.78 square miles (341.31&#160;km<sup>2</sup>), of which, 54.67 square miles (141.59&#160;km<sup>2</sup>) of it is land and 77.11 square miles (199.71&#160;km<sup>2</sup>) is water.<sup id="cite_ref-Gazetteer_files_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gazetteer_files-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The York watershed drains into the <a href="/wiki/York_River_(Maine)" title="York River (Maine)">York River</a>. The highest point in town is <a href="/wiki/Agamenticus" class="mw-redirect" title="Agamenticus">Mount Agamenticus</a>, with an elevation of 692 feet (211 meters) <a href="/wiki/Above_sea_level" class="mw-redirect" title="Above sea level">above sea level</a>. An automobile road travels to the summit, where miles of <a href="/wiki/Hiking" title="Hiking">hiking</a>, <a href="/wiki/Biking" class="mw-redirect" title="Biking">biking</a> and <a href="/wiki/Horse-riding" class="mw-redirect" title="Horse-riding">horse-riding</a> trails are available. The lowest point in town is sea level, along the coastline with the Atlantic Ocean. York lies about 44 miles (71&#160;km) south of <a href="/wiki/Portland,_Maine" title="Portland, Maine">Portland</a>, 98 miles (158&#160;km) south of <a href="/wiki/Augusta,_Maine" title="Augusta, Maine">Augusta</a>, and 65 miles (105&#160;km) north of <a href="/wiki/Boston,_Massachusetts" class="mw-redirect" title="Boston, Massachusetts">Boston</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=York,_Maine&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Passaconaway_Inn,_York_Cliffs,_ME.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a5/Passaconaway_Inn%2C_York_Cliffs%2C_ME.jpg/220px-Passaconaway_Inn%2C_York_Cliffs%2C_ME.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="142" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a5/Passaconaway_Inn%2C_York_Cliffs%2C_ME.jpg/330px-Passaconaway_Inn%2C_York_Cliffs%2C_ME.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a5/Passaconaway_Inn%2C_York_Cliffs%2C_ME.jpg/440px-Passaconaway_Inn%2C_York_Cliffs%2C_ME.jpg 2x" data-file-width="802" data-file-height="516" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Passaconaway_Inn,_York_Cliffs,_ME.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Passaconaway Inn in <a href="/wiki/York_Cliffs,_Maine" title="York Cliffs, Maine">York Cliffs</a> c. 1910</div></div></div>
<p>York School Department receives the largest portion (69%) of the town's <a href="/wiki/Budget" title="Budget">budget</a>. The town of York supports 2,000 students in four schools. Village Elementary School serves grades K-1. Coastal Ridge Elementary School provides education for grades 2-4. York Middle School serves students in grades 5-8, and <a href="/wiki/York_High_School_(Maine)" title="York High School (Maine)">York High School</a> serves students in grades 9-12. <a href="/wiki/Adult_education" title="Adult education">Adult education</a> is also available to York residents.
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2010_census">2010 census</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=York,_Maine&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: 2010 census">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As of the <a href="/wiki/Census" title="Census">census</a><sup id="cite_ref-wwwcensusgov_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wwwcensusgov-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> of 2010, there were 12,529 people, 5,440 households, and 3,601 families living in the town. The <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> was 229.2 inhabitants per square mile (88.5/km<sup>2</sup>). There were 8,649 housing units at an average density of 158.2 per square mile (61.1/km<sup>2</sup>). The racial makeup of the town was 98.6% <a href="/wiki/White_(U.S._Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="White (U.S. Census)">White</a>, 0.4% <a href="/wiki/African_American_(U.S._Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="African American (U.S. Census)">African American</a>, 0.1% <a href="/wiki/Native_American_(U.S._Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Native American (U.S. Census)">Native American</a>, 0.8% <a href="/wiki/Asian_(U.S._Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Asian (U.S. Census)">Asian</a>, 0.3% from <a href="/wiki/Race_(U.S._Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Race (U.S. Census)">other races</a>, and 0.8% from two or more races. <a href="/wiki/Hispanic_(U.S._Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Hispanic (U.S. Census)">Hispanic</a> or <a href="/wiki/Latino_(U.S._Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Latino (U.S. Census)">Latino</a> of any race were 1.0% of the population.
</p><p>There were 5,440 households of which 26.0% had children under the age of 18 living with them, 56.1% were <a href="/wiki/Marriage" title="Marriage">married couples</a> living together, 7.1% had a female householder with no husband present, 3.0% had a male householder with no wife present, and 33.8% were non-families. 28.5% of all households were made up of individuals and 15.1% had someone living alone who was 65 years of age or older. The average household size was 2.30 and the average family size was 2.82.
</p><p>The median age in the town was 49.3 years. 20.2% of residents were under the age of 18; 4.9% were between the ages of 18 and 24; 17.1% were from 25 to 44; 36.3% were from 45 to 64; and 21.5% were 65 years of age or older. The gender makeup of the town was 47.9% male and 52.1% female.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2000_census">2000 census</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=York,_Maine&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: 2000 census">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As of the <a href="/wiki/Census" title="Census">census</a><sup id="cite_ref-GR2_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GR2-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> of 2000, there were 12,854 people, 5,235 households, and 3,690 families living in the town. The <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> was 234.1 people per square mile (90.4/km²). There were 8,053 housing units at an average density of 146.7 per square mile (56.6/km²). The racial makeup of the town was 98.36% <a href="/wiki/White_(U.S._Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="White (U.S. Census)">White</a>, 0.25% <a href="/wiki/African_American_(U.S._Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="African American (U.S. Census)">African American</a>, 0.11% <a href="/wiki/Native_American_(U.S._Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Native American (U.S. Census)">Native American</a>, 0.49% <a href="/wiki/Asian_(U.S._Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Asian (U.S. Census)">Asian</a>, 0.02% <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Islander_(U.S._Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Pacific Islander (U.S. Census)">Pacific Islander</a>, 0.19% from <a href="/wiki/Race_(U.S._Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Race (U.S. Census)">other races</a>, and 0.58% from two or more races. <a href="/wiki/Hispanic_(U.S._Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Hispanic (U.S. Census)">Hispanic</a> or <a href="/wiki/Latino_(U.S._Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Latino (U.S. Census)">Latino</a> of any race were 0.72% of the population.
</p><p>There were 5,235 households out of which 29.5% had children under the age of 18 living with them, 61.1% were <a href="/wiki/Marriage" title="Marriage">married couples</a> living together, 7.0% had a female householder with no husband present, and 29.5% were non-families. 24.0% of all households were made up of individuals and 9.9% had someone living alone who was 65 years of age or older. The average household size was 2.42 and the average family size was 2.88.
</p><p>In the town, the population was spread out with 22.8% under the age of 18, 4.3% from 18 to 24, 25.7% from 25 to 44, 30.1% from 45 to 64, and 17.0% who were 65 years of age or older. The median age was 43 years. For every 100 females, there were 91.8 males. For every 100 females age 18 and over, there were 88.7 males.
</p><p>The median income for a household in the town was $64,000, and the median income for a family was $73,400. Males had a median income of $49,415 versus $31,743 for females. The <a href="/wiki/Per_capita_income" title="Per capita income">per capita income</a> for the town was $30,895. About 1.3% of families and 3.8% of the population were below the <a href="/wiki/Poverty_line" class="mw-redirect" title="Poverty line">poverty line</a>, including 1.8% of those under the age of 18 and 6.7% of those 65 and older.
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Civil_War_monument">Civil War monument</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=York,_Maine&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Civil War monument">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A granite monument depicting a Civil War soldier was erected in 1906 at a traffic triangle in York Village.<sup id="cite_ref-Monument_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Monument-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> It has been suggested by some that his uniform appears to be incorrect for a Union soldier, as he wears a brimmed fedora and goatee; some local tour guides claim that the uniform is actually Confederate.<sup id="cite_ref-Monument_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Monument-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Another explanation is that either the sculptor, Englishman <a href="/wiki/S._H._Barnicoat_Monuments" title="S. H. Barnicoat Monuments">Frederick Barnicoat</a>, or designer John Staples, mistakenly depicted a uniform from the time of the <a href="/wiki/Spanish%E2%80%93American_War" title="Spanish–American War">Spanish–American War</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Monument_16-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Monument-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> It is also possible that the <a href="/wiki/20th_Maine_Volunteer_Infantry_Regiment" title="20th Maine Volunteer Infantry Regiment">20th Maine Volunteer Infantry Regiment</a> did wear a similar uniform.<sup id="cite_ref-Monument_16-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Monument-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
