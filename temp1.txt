<p><b>Fairhope</b> is a city in <a href="/wiki/Baldwin_County,_Alabama" title="Baldwin County, Alabama">Baldwin County</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alabama" title="Alabama">Alabama</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> on the eastern <a href="/wiki/Shoreline" class="mw-redirect" title="Shoreline">shoreline</a> of <a href="/wiki/Mobile_Bay" title="Mobile Bay">Mobile Bay</a>. The <a href="/wiki/2010_United_States_Census" title="2010 United States Census">2010 census</a> lists the population of the city as 15,326.<sup id="cite_ref-Census_2010_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Census_2010-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Fairhope is a principal city of the <a href="/wiki/Daphne-Fairhope-Foley_micropolitan_area" class="mw-redirect" title="Daphne-Fairhope-Foley micropolitan area">Daphne-Fairhope-Foley micropolitan area</a>, which includes all of Baldwin County. In 2016, Fairhope was named the best small town in the <a href="/wiki/Southern_United_States" title="Southern United States">South</a> by <i><a href="/wiki/Southern_Living" title="Southern Living">Southern Living</a></i> magazine.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fairhope,_Alabama&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Fairhope was founded in November 1894 on the site of the former Alabama City as a radical, <a href="/wiki/Utopian_socialist" class="mw-redirect" title="Utopian socialist">utopian socialist</a> <a href="/wiki/Georgism" title="Georgism">Georgist</a> <a href="/wiki/Land_value_tax" title="Land value tax">"Single-Tax"</a> colony by the Fairhope Industrial Association, a group of 28 followers of economist <a href="/wiki/Henry_George" title="Henry George">Henry George</a> who had incorporated earlier that year in <a href="/wiki/Des_Moines,_Iowa" title="Des Moines, Iowa">Des Moines, Iowa</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Their corporate constitution explained their purpose in founding a new colony:
</p>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r960796168">.mw-parser-output .templatequote{overflow:hidden;margin:1em 0;padding:0 40px}.mw-parser-output .templatequote .templatequotecite{line-height:1.5em;text-align:left;padding-left:1.6em;margin-top:0}</style><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>to establish and conduct a model community or colony, free from all forms of private monopoly, and to secure to its members therein equality of opportunity, the full reward of individual efforts, and the benefits of co-operation in matters of general concern.<sup id="cite_ref-fairhopesingletax.com_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fairhopesingletax.com-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup></p></blockquote>
<p>In forming their demonstration project, they pooled their funds to purchase land at "Stapleton's pasture" on the eastern shore of Mobile Bay and then divided it into a number of long-term leaseholds.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> The corporation paid all governmental taxes from rents paid by the lessees, thus simulating a single-tax. The purpose of the single-tax colony was to eliminate disincentives for productive use of land and thereby retain the value of land for the community.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>"Fairhope Avenue" was one of the properties on the 1910 version of the board game <i><a href="/wiki/The_Landlord%27s_Game" title="The Landlord&#39;s Game">The Landlord's Game</a></i>, a precursor of <a href="/wiki/Monopoly_(game)" title="Monopoly (game)"><i>Monopoly</i></a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1907 educator <a href="/wiki/Marietta_Johnson" title="Marietta Johnson">Marietta Johnson</a> founded the School for Organic Education in Fairhope. The school was praised in <a href="/wiki/John_Dewey" title="John Dewey">John Dewey</a>'s influential 1915 book <i>Schools of Tomorrow</i>. Dewey and Johnson were founding members of the <a href="/wiki/Progressive_education" title="Progressive education">Progressive Education Association</a>.
</p><p>Fairhope became a popular wintering spot for artists and intellectuals. <a href="/wiki/Sherwood_Anderson" title="Sherwood Anderson">Sherwood Anderson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Clarence_Darrow" title="Clarence Darrow">Clarence Darrow</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wharton_Esherick" title="Wharton Esherick">Wharton Esherick</a>, Carl Zigrosser, and <a href="/wiki/Upton_Sinclair" title="Upton Sinclair">Upton Sinclair</a> were among its notable visitors.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Fairhope_Municipal_Pier_Fountain.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/31/Fairhope_Municipal_Pier_Fountain.JPG/220px-Fairhope_Municipal_Pier_Fountain.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/31/Fairhope_Municipal_Pier_Fountain.JPG/330px-Fairhope_Municipal_Pier_Fountain.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/31/Fairhope_Municipal_Pier_Fountain.JPG/440px-Fairhope_Municipal_Pier_Fountain.JPG 2x" data-file-width="2848" data-file-height="2136" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Fairhope_Municipal_Pier_Fountain.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Fountain at the Municipal Pier</div></div></div>
<p>The Fairhope Single-Tax Corporation still operates, with 1,800 leaseholds covering more than 4,000 acres (16&#160;km<sup>2</sup>) in and around the current city of Fairhope. Despite the ideals of the corporation, the town has transitioned from utopian experiment to artists' and intellectuals' colony to boutique resort and affluent suburb of <a href="/wiki/Mobile,_Alabama" title="Mobile, Alabama">Mobile</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-fairhopesingletax.com_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fairhopesingletax.com-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>For over 50 years, fishermen and residents of Fairhope have experienced the "jubilee" phenomenon. During a <a href="/wiki/Mobile_Bay_jubilee" title="Mobile Bay jubilee">jubilee</a> along the shores of Mobile Bay, some aquatic animals, including <a href="/wiki/Crab" title="Crab">blue crabs</a>, <a href="/wiki/Flounder" title="Flounder">flounder</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stingray" title="Stingray">stingrays</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Eel" title="Eel">eels</a>, come to the shallow water. At those times, it is possible to catch the fish, crabs, and other sea life near the water's edge.
</p><p>The <a href="/w/index.php?title=Weeks_Bay_Nature_Reserve&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Weeks Bay Nature Reserve (page does not exist)">Weeks Bay Nature Reserve</a>, located 10 miles (16&#160;km) to the southeast, is known for the many oaks, wildlife and <a href="/wiki/Pitcher_plant" title="Pitcher plant">pitcher plants</a> along the elevated walkways through the swamp forest.
</p><p>The Bell Building on the <a href="/wiki/Faulkner_State_Community_College" class="mw-redirect" title="Faulkner State Community College">Faulkner State Community College</a> campus houses the Marietta Johnson Museum.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> The Fairhope Museum of History is located downtown.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fairhope,_Alabama&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Fairhope is located on the shore of Mobile Bay. It is located 6 miles (10&#160;km) south of <a href="/wiki/Daphne,_Alabama" title="Daphne, Alabama">Daphne</a> and 10 miles (16&#160;km) south of <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Fort,_Alabama" title="Spanish Fort, Alabama">Spanish Fort</a>. <a href="/wiki/U.S._Route_98" title="U.S. Route 98">U.S. Route 98</a> (Greeno Road) runs north–south through the city. It lies on a sloping plateau.
</p><p>According to the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Census_Bureau" title="United States Census Bureau">U.S. Census Bureau</a>, the city has a total area of 12.0 square miles (31.1&#160;km<sup>2</sup>), of which 0.019 square mile (0.05&#160;km<sup>2</sup>), or 0.16%, is water.<sup id="cite_ref-Census_2010_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Census_2010-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Its elevation ranges from sea level at the bay to 122 feet (37&#160;m) in the city center.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fairhope,_Alabama&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Fairhope has a <a href="/wiki/Humid_subtropical_climate" title="Humid subtropical climate">humid subtropical climate</a>. It experiences hot, humid summers and generally mild winters, with average temperatures ranging from 81.9&#160;°F (27.7&#160;°C) in the summer to 50.4&#160;°F (10.2&#160;°C) during winter.

<p>As of the <a href="/wiki/Census" title="Census">census</a> of 2010, there were 15,326 people, 6,732 households, and 4,395 families residing in the city. Its <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> was 1,271 per square mile (491/km<sup>2</sup>). There were 7,659 housing units at an average density of 634.5 per square mile (245.0/km<sup>2</sup>). The racial makeup of the city was 91.1% <a href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Race (United States Census)">White</a>, 6.2% <a href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Race (United States Census)">Black</a>, 0.7% <a href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Race (United States Census)">Asian</a>, 0.2% <a href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Race (United States Census)">Native American</a>, 0.0% <a href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Race (United States Census)">Pacific Islander</a>, 0.9% from <a href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Race (United States Census)">other races</a>, and 0.8% from two or more races. 2.8% of the population were <a href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Race (United States Census)">Hispanic</a> or <a href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Race (United States Census)">Latino</a> of any race.
</p><p>There were 6,732 households out of which 25.9% had children under the age of 18 living with them, 53.6% were <a href="/wiki/Marriage" title="Marriage">married couples</a> living together, 9.1% had a female householder with no husband present, and 34.7% were non-families. 31.2% of all households were made up of individuals and 17.1% had someone living alone who was 65 years of age or older. The average household size was 2.26 and the average family size was 2.84.
</p><p>21.4% of the population was under the age of 18, 4.9% from 18 to 24, 20.4% from 25 to 44, 28.5% from 45 to 64, and 23.7% who were 65 years of age or older. The median age was 46 years. For every 100 females, there were 86.4 males. For every 100 females age 18 and over, there were 86.3 males. The median income for a household in the city was $66,157, and the median income for a family was $93,549. Males had a median income of $60,591 versus $36,218 for females. The <a href="/wiki/Per_capita_income" title="Per capita income">per capita income</a> for the city was $35,086. About 5.0% of families and 5.9% of the population were below the <a href="/wiki/Poverty_line" class="mw-redirect" title="Poverty line">poverty line</a>, including 9.8% of those under age 18 and 4.6% of those age 65 or over.
</p>

<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Episcopal_Church_in_Fairhope,_Alabama.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/49/Episcopal_Church_in_Fairhope%2C_Alabama.png/220px-Episcopal_Church_in_Fairhope%2C_Alabama.png" decoding="async" width="220" height="304" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/49/Episcopal_Church_in_Fairhope%2C_Alabama.png/330px-Episcopal_Church_in_Fairhope%2C_Alabama.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/49/Episcopal_Church_in_Fairhope%2C_Alabama.png/440px-Episcopal_Church_in_Fairhope%2C_Alabama.png 2x" data-file-width="519" data-file-height="716" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Episcopal_Church_in_Fairhope,_Alabama.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Episcopal Church in Fairhope, Alabama</div></div></div>
<p>Local and national real estate developers have built commercial facilities in the downtown area that are larger than have been historically allowed.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Fairhope's building and <a href="/wiki/Zoning_ordinance" class="mw-redirect" title="Zoning ordinance">zoning ordinances</a> overlap with those of Baldwin County. Residents of the city want more control of construction projects near, but still outside the city limits, while residents outside the city limits want less city control of their property.<sup id="cite_ref-zoning_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-zoning-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fairhope,_Alabama&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

</p>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Fairhope_High_School" title="Fairhope High School">Fairhope High School</a> (9–12)&#160;&#8211;&#32;1,142 students, Principal Jon Cardwell.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> Fairhope High School is located in the southernmost part of Fairhope. It is estimated that 39% of the city of Fairhope's high school age students attend <a href="/wiki/Daphne_High_School" title="Daphne High School">Daphne High School</a> that is north of Fairhope.</li>
<li>Fairhope Middle School (7–8)&#160;&#8211;&#32;642 students, Principal Angie Hall</li>

<li>Fairhope Intermediate School (4–6)&#160;&#8211;&#32;397 students, Princial</li>
<li>Fairhope Elementary School (K–3)&#160;&#8211;&#32;278 students, Principal</li></ul>

The school offers education to life groups that traditionally span 1st through 8th grades.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/St._Michael_Catholic_High_School_(Alabama)" title="St. Michael Catholic High School (Alabama)">St. Michael Catholic High School</a>&#160;&#8211;&#32;beginning in 2016 with grades 9–10, adding a grade each year thereafter for a total enrollment between 360–400</li>

<li>The <a href="/wiki/University_of_South_Alabama" title="University of South Alabama">University of South Alabama</a>&#160;&#8211;&#32;has a branch campus in Fairhope providing graduate and upper-level undergraduate courses in education, counseling, nursing and business alongside non-credit and community service programs</li></ul>
