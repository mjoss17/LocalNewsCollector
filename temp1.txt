<p><b>Phenix City</b> is a city in <a href="/wiki/Lee_County,_Alabama" title="Lee County, Alabama">Lee</a> and <a href="/wiki/Russell_County,_Alabama" title="Russell County, Alabama">Russell</a> counties in the U.S. state of <a href="/wiki/Alabama" title="Alabama">Alabama</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/County_seat" title="County seat">county seat</a> of Russell County.<sup id="cite_ref-GR6_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GR6-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> As of the <a href="/wiki/2010_United_States_Census" title="2010 United States Census">2010 census</a>, the population of the city was 32,822.
</p><p>Phenix City lies immediately west across the <a href="/wiki/Chattahoochee_River" title="Chattahoochee River">Chattahoochee River</a> from the much larger <a href="/wiki/Columbus,_Georgia" title="Columbus, Georgia">Columbus, Georgia</a> and observes <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Time_Zone_(North_America)" class="mw-redirect" title="Eastern Time Zone (North America)">Eastern Time</a> on a <i>de facto</i> basis (in contrast to the rest of Alabama, which observes <a href="/wiki/Central_Time_Zone_(North_America)" class="mw-redirect" title="Central Time Zone (North America)">Central Time</a>) due to Phenix City's strong economic ties to Columbus. Most of Phenix City is included in the <a href="/wiki/Columbus,_Georgia_metropolitan_area" class="mw-redirect" title="Columbus, Georgia metropolitan area">Columbus Metropolitan Statistical Area</a>, but a section is located in Lee County, and is therefore in the <a href="/wiki/Auburn,_Alabama" title="Auburn, Alabama">Auburn, Alabama</a> Metropolitan Statistical Area. The entire city is part of the <a href="/wiki/Columbus%E2%80%93Auburn%E2%80%93Opelika,_GA%E2%80%93AL_CSA" title="Columbus–Auburn–Opelika, GA–AL CSA">Columbus-Auburn-Opelika Combined Statistical Area</a>.
</p><p>In 2007, <i><a href="/wiki/BusinessWeek" class="mw-redirect" title="BusinessWeek">BusinessWeek</a></i> named Phenix City the nation's No. 1 Best Affordable Suburb to raise a family.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>It is home to <a href="/wiki/Chattahoochee_Valley_Community_College" title="Chattahoochee Valley Community College">Chattahoochee Valley Community College</a>. This is a public, two-year college with an acceptance rate of 100%.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As of 2020, the mayor is <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Lowe_(Canadian_football)" title="Eddie Lowe (Canadian football)">Eddie N. Lowe</a>, the city's first black mayor elected by the public. The city manager, who holds the task of organizational matters, is Wallace Hunter.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Phenix_City,_Alabama&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Phenix City is located at <span class="plainlinks nourlexpansion"><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="//geohack.toolforge.org/geohack.php?pagename=Phenix_City,_Alabama&amp;params=32_28_22_N_85_1_12_W_type:city"><span class="geo-default"><span class="geo-dms" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location"><span class="latitude">32°28′22″N</span> <span class="longitude">85°1′12″W</span></span></span><span class="geo-multi-punct">&#xfeff; / &#xfeff;</span><span class="geo-nondefault"><span class="geo-dec" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location">32.47278°N 85.02000°W</span><span style="display:none">&#xfeff; / <span class="geo">32.47278; -85.02000</span></span></span></a></span> (32.472822, −85.020121).<sup id="cite_ref-GR1_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GR1-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> It is the easternmost settlement in the state of Alabama as well as the <a href="/wiki/Central_Time_Zone" title="Central Time Zone">Central Time Zone</a>, but it and some other nearby areas unofficially observe Eastern Time, as these areas are part of the metropolitan area of the considerably larger city of Columbus, Georgia, which is in the <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Time_Zone" title="Eastern Time Zone">Eastern Time Zone</a> and adjacent to the city across the <a href="/wiki/Chattahoochee_River" title="Chattahoochee River">Chattahoochee River</a> to the east.<sup id="cite_ref-WR1_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WR1-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Many major highways run through Phenix City, namely U.S. Routes <a href="/wiki/U.S._Route_80_in_Alabama" title="U.S. Route 80 in Alabama">80</a>, <a href="/wiki/U.S._Route_280" title="U.S. Route 280">280</a>, and <a href="/wiki/U.S._Route_431_in_Alabama" title="U.S. Route 431 in Alabama">431</a>. U.S. 80 runs through the northern and western parts of the city, leading west 44&#160;mi (71&#160;km) to <a href="/wiki/Tuskegee,_Alabama" title="Tuskegee, Alabama">Tuskegee</a> and
northeast to Columbus, Georgia. U.S. Route 280 runs through the western part of the city from northwest to southeast, leading northwest 27&#160;mi (43&#160;km) to <a href="/wiki/Opelika,_Alabama" title="Opelika, Alabama">Opelika</a> and southeast into Columbus. U.S. Route 431 runs north to south to the west of the city, concurrent with US 280, which it follows to Opelika, and leads south 47&#160;mi (76&#160;km) to <a href="/wiki/Eufaula,_Alabama" title="Eufaula, Alabama">Eufaula</a>.
</p><p>According to the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Census_Bureau" title="United States Census Bureau">U.S. Census Bureau</a>, the city has a total area of 24.8 square miles (64&#160;km<sup>2</sup>), of which 24.6 square miles (64&#160;km<sup>2</sup>) is land and 0.2 square miles (0.52&#160;km<sup>2</sup>) (0.61%) is water.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Phenix_City,_Alabama&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:New_Russell_County_Courthouse.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/ce/New_Russell_County_Courthouse.JPG/220px-New_Russell_County_Courthouse.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="160" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/ce/New_Russell_County_Courthouse.JPG/330px-New_Russell_County_Courthouse.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/ce/New_Russell_County_Courthouse.JPG/440px-New_Russell_County_Courthouse.JPG 2x" data-file-width="2883" data-file-height="2091" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:New_Russell_County_Courthouse.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Russell_County,_Alabama" title="Russell County, Alabama">Russell County</a> Courthouse in Phenix City</div></div></div><p>What some claim was the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Columbus,_Georgia" class="mw-redirect" title="Battle of Columbus, Georgia">last battle of the Civil War</a> took place in Phenix City, then known as <a href="/wiki/Girard,_Alabama" title="Girard, Alabama">Girard</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>What is Phenix City today initially began as two towns bordering each other on the North and South. On the south side was Girard, in Russell County, and the town of Brownville, on the north side, which was entirely within <a href="/wiki/Lee_County,_Alabama" title="Lee County, Alabama">Lee County</a>. Because a community in <a href="/wiki/Tuscaloosa_County,_Alabama" title="Tuscaloosa County, Alabama">Tuscaloosa County</a> already claimed the name of <a href="/wiki/Brownville,_Alabama" title="Brownville, Alabama">Brownville</a>, the post office insisted on the name of "Lively." To compound the confusion, the nearby railroad depot was called Knight's Station. Because residents preferred Brownville, on the 1880 U.S. Census, it was listed as both Brownville and Lively. It was formally incorporated by the legislature on February 23, 1883.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Within the decade, the name would be changed to Phenix (or Phenix City), although some misspellings had it as "Phoenix." It was not clear if it was named for Phenix Mills in adjacent Columbus, Georgia, or for the <a href="/wiki/Phoenix_(mythology)" title="Phoenix (mythology)">fabled bird</a>. It appeared as Phenix City on the 1890 U.S. Census and was incorporated as such on February 19, 1897 by the state legislature. The City Official Website displays the mythical Phoenix. The City's Seal also depicts the Phoenix burning up in its nest. The 'Our Community/History' page confirms the uncertainty behind the name: "<i>Although no definitive source reveals why</i> <i>this name was chosen</i>."
</p><p>On August 9, 1923, Russell County's neighboring Girard (which made up most of current downtown Phenix City) and Lee County's Phenix City formally merged, keeping the name of Phenix City. Because the consolidated city still remained divided into two counties, in 1932, the Lee County portion was moved entirely into Russell County (also cited for the redraw was to account for population shifts involved in the <a href="/wiki/Auburn_University" title="Auburn University">Auburn University</a> opening and expansion). Lee County received the rural segment centered on <a href="/wiki/Marvyn,_Alabama" title="Marvyn, Alabama">Marvyn</a> as compensation, which was formerly in Russell County's northwest corner.
</p><p>In 1926, the Russell County portion of Phenix City was designated the second county seat (the seat from 1868 had been at rural <a href="/wiki/Seale,_Alabama" title="Seale, Alabama">Seale</a>). In 1934/35, Phenix City then became sole county seat.
</p><p>Phenix City was notorious during the 1940s and 1950s for being a haven for organized crime, prostitution, and gambling. Many of its customers came from the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Army" title="United States Army">United States Army</a> training center at <a href="/wiki/Fort_Benning" title="Fort Benning">Fort Benning</a>, <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(U.S._state)" title="Georgia (U.S. state)">Georgia</a>. The leaders of the crime syndicate in Phenix City were Jimmie Matthews and Hoyt Shepherd. <a href="/wiki/Albert_Patterson" title="Albert Patterson">Albert Patterson</a>, from Phenix City, was elected to become attorney general of Alabama on a platform of reforming the city, but was shot and killed in 1954 outside his office on 5th Ave, N of 14th Street.   As a result, the city had a negative reputation, and many people still associate this legacy with Phenix City. <i>The Tragedy and the Triumph of Phenix City, Alabama</i> by Margaret Ann Barnes chronicles these events, which led the small town to be known as "Sin City, USA". The bordertown was the subject of an acclaimed film, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Phenix_City_Story" title="The Phenix City Story">The Phenix City Story</a></i>, made in 1955.
</p><p>In 1955, it won the <a href="/wiki/All-America_City_Award" title="All-America City Award">All-America City Award</a> from the <a href="/wiki/National_Municipal_League" class="mw-redirect" title="National Municipal League">National Municipal League</a>.
</p><p>Curiously, despite the city having been entirely annexed into Russell County in 1932, the growth of the city has spread northward back into Lee County, where it first appeared on 1980 U.S. Census records. As of 2010<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Phenix_City,_Alabama&amp;action=edit">&#91;update&#93;</a></sup>, nearly 4,200 residents (out of almost 33,000) reside in the Lee County portion, almost exactly the same number that lived in Phenix City in 1910 (which was then entirely in Lee County).<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>As of the census of 2000, there were 28,265 people, 11,517 households, and 7,566 families residing in the city. The population density was 1,149.1 people per square mile (443.6/km²). There were 13,250 housing units at an average density of 538.7 per square mile (208.0/km²). The racial makeup of the city was 52.94% <a href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Race (United States Census)">White</a>, 44.97% <a href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Race (United States Census)">Black</a> or <a href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Race (United States Census)">African American</a>, 0.23% <a href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Race (United States Census)">Native American</a>, 0.53% <a href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Race (United States Census)">Asian</a>, 0.02% <a href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Race (United States Census)">Pacific Islander</a>, 0.56% from <a href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Race (United States Census)">other races</a>, and 0.76% from two or more races. 1.49% of the population were <a href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Race (United States Census)">Hispanic</a> or <a href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Race (United States Census)">Latino</a> of any race.
</p><p>There were 11,517 households out of which 31.7% had children under the age of 18 living with them, 39.4% were married couples living together, 22.1% had a female householder with no husband present, and 34.3% were non-families. 30.4% of all households were made up of individuals and 11.5% had someone living alone who was 65 years of age or older. The average household size was 2.40 and the average family size was 2.99.
</p><p>In the city, the population was spread out with 26.3% under the age of 18, 9.6% from 18 to 24, 28.7% from 25 to 44, 21.2% from 45 to 64, and 14.2% who were 65 years of age or older. The median age was 35 years. For every 100 females, there were 86.2 males. For every 100 females age 18 and over, there were 80.4 males.
</p><p>The median income for a household in the city was $26,720, and the median income for a family was $33,740. Males had a median income of $28,906 versus $21,348 for females. The per capita income for the city was $14,619. About 18.8% of families and 21.3% of the population were below the poverty line, including 29.5% of those under age 18 and 20.6% of those age 65 or over.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2010_census">2010 census</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Phenix_City,_Alabama&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2010 census">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As of the census of 2010, there were 32,822 people, 13,243 households, and 8,623 families residing in the city. The population density was 1,334.2 people per square mile (515.3/km²). There were 15,198 housing units at an average density of 617.8 per square mile (238.6/km²). The racial makeup of the city was 48.7% <a href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Race (United States Census)">White</a>, 46.6% <a href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Race (United States Census)">Black</a> or <a href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Race (United States Census)">African American</a>, 0.3% <a href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Race (United States Census)">Native American</a>, 0.7% <a href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Race (United States Census)">Asian</a>, 0.2% <a href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Race (United States Census)">Pacific Islander</a>, 1.4% from <a href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Race (United States Census)">other races</a>, and 2.2% from two or more races. 4.0% of the population were <a href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Race (United States Census)">Hispanic</a> or <a href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Race (United States Census)">Latino</a> of any race.
</p><p>There were 13,243 households out of which 31.8% had children under the age of 18 living with them, 36.4% were married couples living together, 23.9% had a female householder with no husband present, and 34.9% were non-families. 30.1% of all households were made up of individuals and 9.5% had someone living alone who was 65 years of age or older. The average household size was 2.44 and the average family size was 3.03.
</p><p>In the city, the population was spread out with 26.9% under the age of 18, 10.4% from 18 to 24, 27.9% from 25 to 44, 23.0% from 45 to 64, and 11.8% who were 65 years of age or older. The median age was 33.1 years. For every 100 females, there were 87.7 males. For every 100 females age 18 and over, there were 86.0 males.
</p><p>The median income for a household in the city was $33,120, and the median income for a family was $39,417. Males had a median income of $36,827 versus $27,001 for females. The per capita income for the city was $18,883. About 20.3% of families and 23.9% of the population were below the poverty line, including 39.7% of those under age 18 and 13.1% of those age 65 or over.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Time_zone">Time zone</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Phenix_City,_Alabama&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Time zone">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Unlike the rest of Alabama which lies in the <a href="/wiki/Central_Time_Zone_(North_America)" class="mw-redirect" title="Central Time Zone (North America)">Central Time Zone</a>, Phenix City's proximity to the larger city of neighboring <a href="/wiki/Columbus,_Georgia" title="Columbus, Georgia">Columbus, Georgia</a>, located in the <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Time_Zone_(North_America)" class="mw-redirect" title="Eastern Time Zone (North America)">Eastern Time Zone</a>, and its strong economic links leads Phenix City (including its municipal government) and localities within a 10–15&#160;mi (16–24&#160;km) radius (such as <a href="/wiki/Smiths_Station,_Alabama" title="Smiths Station, Alabama">Smiths Station</a>) to observe <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Time_Zone" title="Eastern Time Zone">Eastern Time</a> on a <i>de facto</i> basis.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Media">Media</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Phenix_City,_Alabama&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Media">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/Media_in_Columbus,_Georgia" title="Media in Columbus, Georgia">Media in Columbus, Georgia</a></div>

There are, however, three radio stations licensed in Phenix City: <a href="/wiki/WURY-LP" title="WURY-LP">WURY-LP</a> (97.1 FM), <a href="/wiki/WHTY_(FM)" title="WHTY (FM)">WHTY</a> (100.1 FM), and <a href="/wiki/WGSY" title="WGSY">WGSY</a> (1460 AM).
</p>
