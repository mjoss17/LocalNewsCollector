	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Trib Publications</h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
		<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"><span class="mw-redirectedfrom">&#160;&#160;(Redirected from <a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Western_Star_(Bessemer,_Alabama)&amp;redirect=no" class="mw-redirect" title="The Western Star (Bessemer, Alabama)">The Western Star (Bessemer, Alabama)</a>)</span></div>

		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="shortdescription nomobile noexcerpt noprint searchaux" style="display:none">Newspaper in Manchester, Georgia</div>
<p><b>Trib Publications</b> is a regional newspaper chain based in <a href="/wiki/Manchester,_Georgia" title="Manchester, Georgia">Manchester, Georgia</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-oakley1_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-oakley1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Trib Publications was started by Robert Tribble in 1968.  Tribble, previously the editor of the local <a href="/wiki/Weekly_newspaper" title="Weekly newspaper">weekly newspaper</a> <i>Manchester Mercury</i>, bought three small weeklies, the <i>Harris County Journal</i>, <i>Meriwether Vindicator</i> and <i>Talbotton New Era</i> with a total circulation of 3,500. In 1976 he bought his former paper, by then called the <i>Manchester Star-Mercury</i>.  By 1984 he had 15 papers, which grew to 30 by 1995.<sup id="cite_ref-epone_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-epone-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>  Some of Trib's earliest Georgia acquisitions were sold in 1999 to another small paper aggregator, Millard B. Grimes of <a href="/wiki/Athens,_Georgia" title="Athens, Georgia">Athens, Georgia</a>'s Grimes Publications.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>  Trib re-acquired these properties from Grimes in 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Among Trib's other papers is <i>The Western Star</i>, a weekly in <a href="/wiki/Bessemer,_Alabama" title="Bessemer, Alabama">Bessemer, Alabama</a>. The <i>Star</i> was established in 1984,<sup id="cite_ref-founding_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-founding-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-oakley1_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-oakley1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> but its predecessor titles go back to 1887 (<i>Bessemer Advertiser</i> 1949-1990, <i>Tribune-Advertiser</i> 1930-1949, <i>Bessemer Advertiser</i> 1920-1930, <i>Bessemer Weekly</i> 1889-1920, <i>The Bessemer</i> 1887-1889).<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>  As of 1995, its circulation was 9,500, at that time the largest circulation among Trib's papers;<sup id="cite_ref-epone_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-epone-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> the circulation has since dropped to less than 3,000.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The <i>Star'</i>s offices in Charleston House are on the <a href="/wiki/National_Register_of_Historic_Places" title="National Register of Historic Places">National Register of Historic Places</a> as part of the Downtown Bessemer Historic District.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Trib has continued to acquire new properties, buying the <i>Houston Home Journal</i> of <a href="/wiki/Perry,_Georgia" title="Perry, Georgia">Perry, Georgia</a> in 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
